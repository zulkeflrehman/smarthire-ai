What is SmartHire AI?

Your AI-powered hiring assistant — explained in plain English

SmartHire AI is a tool that automatically reads and ranks job applicants' resumes so you don't have to read them all yourself. You paste a job description, upload CVs, and the AI scores every candidate and sorts them from best match to worst — in seconds.

How to Use It — Step by Step
{% for step in steps %}{% endfor %}
1
Create a Job Posting
Click New Job in the sidebar. Fill in the job title, paste the full job description, and list the required skills (e.g. Python, Django, SQL). This tells the AI what the perfect candidate looks like.
2
Upload Resumes
Click Upload Resumes on the job page. Drag and drop up to 50 PDF or DOCX files at once. The system automatically extracts the candidate's name, email, skills, education, and experience from each file.
3
Run the AI Screening
Click Screen Resumes. The AI pipeline runs in seconds. It reads every resume, compares it to your job description using three different AI signals, and gives each candidate a score from 0–100.
4
Review the Ranked Results
See all candidates ranked #1 to #N by their match score. Use the search box to find specific candidates, use the score filter to focus on top matches, or click the chart bars to filter by score range. Click any row to expand it and see that candidate's full skill breakdown inline.
5
Open the AI Profile (Optional)
Click AI Profile to open the full candidate page. There you can:
  • Generate an AI written evaluation — strengths, weaknesses, recommendation
  • Get 10 tailored interview questions based on their resume
  • Chat with the AI — ask anything like "Why is this person a good fit?" or "What skills are they missing?"
6
Shortlist or Reject
Use the Status dropdown on each candidate row to mark them as Shortlisted ✓, Rejected ✗, or leave as Pending. Changes save instantly — no page reload needed.
7
Export Results
Click Export CSV at the top right to download all results as a spreadsheet — including scores, skills, education, experience, and AI recommendation.
How the Scoring Works — Simple Explanation

Every resume gets a score from 0 to 100. The score is calculated using three different AI signals, each looking at the resume from a different angle:

BERT
45%
Semantic Meaning Match
This is the smartest signal. It reads the meaning of the resume, not just the exact words. So if the job says "build REST APIs" and the resume says "developed web services", BERT knows they mean the same thing. It's like having someone who actually understands context, not just keywords.
TF-IDF
35%
Keyword Relevance Match
This signal counts how many important words from the job description also appear in the resume, and gives more weight to words that are rare and specific (like a technology name) versus common words (like "the" or "and"). Classic, fast, and reliable.
Skills
20%
Direct Skill Match
This simply checks: of the required skills you listed (e.g. Python, SQL, React), how many appear in this resume? A candidate with 8 out of 10 required skills scores higher than one with only 3 out of 10. Simple and transparent.
On the free hosted version (Render.com), BERT is disabled to stay within 512MB RAM. Scoring uses TF-IDF + Skills only, which is still highly accurate for most roles.
Frequently Asked Questions
{% for faq in faqs %}{% endfor %}
🔒 Is my data private?
Resume files are stored on your own server. The only external call is to the Groq API when you use the AI chat feature — only the resume text (not the file) is sent. If you run locally without a GROQ_API_KEY, nothing ever leaves your computer.
❓ Does it work without an API key?
Yes! Resume scoring, ranking, filtering, and CSV export work completely without a GROQ API key. The key is only needed for the AI chat, interview questions, and written evaluations. Get a free key at console.groq.com — takes 2 minutes.
📁 What file types does it support?
PDF and DOCX (Microsoft Word) only. Scanned image PDFs (where the text is a photo) may not parse correctly — use text-based PDFs for best results.
📤 How many resumes can I upload at once?
Up to 50 files per upload batch. You can run multiple uploads for the same job. Each upload batch is processed immediately.
⚡ Why is the live site slow to load the first time?
The live demo runs on Render.com's free plan, which sleeps after 15 minutes of no activity. The first visit after sleeping takes ~30 seconds to wake up. After that, it's fast. Upgrade to a paid plan to avoid this.
💰 Is this free?
100% free and open source (MIT license). The Groq API has a generous free tier (6,000 requests/day). Render.com's free hosting is sufficient for personal and small team use.
